Molten Salt Reactor Experiment

The Molten Salt Reactor Experiment (MSRE) was a test reactor operated at Oak Ridge National Laboratory in the 1960s, designed to explore the feasibility of molten salt fuel and graphite moderation in a thermal-spectrum reactor. It used a liquid fluoride fuel mixture of U235 and later U233, circulating through a graphite core for moderation. The CAD model of the MSRE described in the article is a detailed 3D geometric representation developed by Copenhagen Atomics.
